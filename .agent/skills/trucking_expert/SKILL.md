# Trucking Expert Skill

You are an expert in the trucking and freight logistics industry. Use this knowledge when the user asks about trucking, dispatch, or transportation topics.

## FMCSA Regulations (Key Points)

### Hours of Service (HOS)
- **11-Hour Driving Limit**: May drive a maximum of 11 hours after 10 consecutive hours off duty.
- **14-Hour Limit**: May not drive beyond the 14th consecutive hour after coming on duty.
- **30-Minute Break**: Required after 8 cumulative hours of driving.
- **60/70-Hour Limit**: May not drive after 60/70 hours on duty in 7/8 consecutive days.
- **Sleeper Berth**: Must be at least 7 consecutive hours in sleeper berth, plus 2 consecutive hours either in sleeper, off-duty, or combination.

### ELD (Electronic Logging Device)
- Required for most commercial motor vehicles (CMVs).
- Must be registered with FMCSA.
- Records driving time automatically when vehicle is in motion.

### Driver Qualifications
- CDL (Commercial Driver's License) required for vehicles over 26,001 lbs.
- Medical Examiner's Certificate (DOT physical) required every 2 years.
- Drug and alcohol testing required (pre-employment, random, post-accident).

## Dispatch Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Deadhead** | Miles driven empty (no load). Not paid. |
| **Detention** | Time waiting at shipper/receiver beyond free time (usually 2 hours). |
| **Lumper** | Third-party hired to load/unload freight. |
| **BOL (Bill of Lading)** | Legal document between shipper and carrier. |
| **Rate Confirmation** | Written agreement on load price and terms. |
| **Broker** | Middleman connecting shippers with carriers. |
| **Factoring** | Selling invoices for immediate cash (minus fee). |
| **Per Diem** | Daily allowance for driver expenses (tax deductible). |
| **Layover** | Waiting period between loads. |
| **Hotshot** | Expedited/time-sensitive loads. |
| **Reefer** | Refrigerated trailer. |
| **Flatbed** | Open trailer for oversized/heavy loads. |
| **Dry Van** | Standard enclosed trailer. |

## Load Board Platforms
- **DAT Load Board**: Industry standard, largest network.
- **Truckstop.com**: Good for owner-operators.
- **123Loadboard**: Budget option.
- **Convoy**: Digital freight network (no negotiations).
- **Uber Freight**: App-based booking.

## Rate Calculation
- **Rule of Thumb**: $2.50-$3.50 per mile (varies by lane, fuel, season).
- **Fuel Surcharge**: Typically tied to DOE national average.
- **Accessorial Charges**: Detention, lumper fees, TONU (Truck Ordered Not Used).

## MC Authority
- **MC Number**: Required for interstate for-hire carriers.
- **DOT Number**: Required for all commercial vehicles in interstate commerce.
- **BOC-3**: Process agent designation (required for MC authority).
- **Insurance Minimums**: $750,000 liability (general freight), $1M (hazmat).
